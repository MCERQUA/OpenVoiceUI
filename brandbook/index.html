01 - Brand Identity
The face that listens.
OpenVoiceUI is defined by the eye avatar, neon-on-dark aesthetics, and a cyberpunk-meets-clinical sensibility. The eyes ARE the logo.
Mark

The Eyes

Two animated eyes with blue glow, expressive pupils, and mood states. The eyes are the logo. No wordmark needed in small contexts.

Wordmark

OpenVoiceUI

Space Grotesk 700. "Voice" rendered in primary blue (#0088ff). Always one word, never hyphenated.

Personality

Ambient Intelligence

Always listening, never intrusive. The UI is dark, the feedback is subtle, the voice is the primary interface.

02 - Logo Discovery
Constructing the mark.
The eye avatar is built from layered ellipses with radial gradients, a blue glow filter, and pupil highlights for dimensionality.
1. Glow field
2. Sclera
3. Pupil
4. Highlights

Why eyes? Voice interfaces have no visible output. The eyes give the AI a face without a body. They express state (listening, thinking, speaking, error) through blinks, pupil dilation, and mood shapes. The user always knows what the system is doing.

07 - Motion
Ambient, not performative.
Animations serve feedback, not decoration. Eye blinks confirm attention. Glow pulses indicate state.
Eye Blink

0.15s ease

Eyelid close/open. Confirms the system heard you.

Panel Slide

0.3s ease

Settings, transcript panels slide in from edges.

Status Pulse

0.8s infinite

Listening indicator. Rhythmic feedback during active state.

Glow Breathe

3s infinite

Eye glow intensity. Ambient presence when idle.

Rules: Never exceed 0.3s for UI transitions. Looping animations must have a purpose. Disable all non-essential animations on Raspberry Pi via pi-overrides.css.
